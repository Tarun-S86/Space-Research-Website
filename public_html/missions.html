Missions

Mercury

Mercury missions, such as MESSENGER, have revealed the planet's extreme temperature variations and surface features.

Venus

Venus missions like Venera and Akatsuki have explored its dense atmosphere and extreme surface conditions.

Mars missions have been critical in studying the planet's geology and the search for past life, including rovers like Curiosity.

Venus

Venera Program (Soviet Union): A series of missions from 1961 to 1984, including Venera 7 (1970), the first successful lander on Venus.
Magellan (USA): Orbited Venus from 1990 to 1994, mapping its surface with radar.
Akatsuki (Japan): Launched in 2010, it is still orbiting Venus, studying its atmosphere.

Mars

Mariner 4: The first successful mission to Mars in 1965.
Viking Program: Two missions in 1976, Viking 1 and Viking 2, which included orbiters and landers.
Mars Pathfinder: A successful mission in 1997, with the Sojourner rover.
Mars Rovers: Multiple rovers, including Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance, have landed on Mars to study its surface.
Mars Orbiters: Missions such as Mars Odyssey (2001), Mars Express (2003), and MAVEN (2014) have been studying the planet from orbit.
